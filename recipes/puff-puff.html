Puff-Puff Recipe

Puff-puff is deep-fried yeasted dough. This delicious snack is popular in West African cuisine.

It is commonly served at breakfast or dinner, with fried hot pepper sauce and/or fried beans.

Ingredients for About 50 Puff-puff Balls

  • 1 kg All-purpose flour
  • 1 L Milk
  • 14 g Fast-acting yeast
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 250 g of sugar
  • 10 g vanilla sugar
  • 2 L vegetable oil or other oil for deep frying

Directions

  1. Mix all the dry ingredients (salt, sugar, vanilla sugar, flour, yeast) in a big bowl, pot, or container (5 to 10L) and mix well.
  2. Add the milk and mix with your hands until you get a consistent wet smooth batter.
  3. Put a lid on the bowl/container and let it stand at room temperature for 4-5 hours for the batter to rise (to about 2X).
  4. Heat the oil (medium-high setting) in a deep fryer (or deep pot) till the oil is hot enough for frying.
  5. Mix the batter again with your hands for about a minute.
  6. Drop small quantities of dough into the hot oil and fry until golden brown. See the technique in the video for dropping batter in oil.
  7. Fry in batches of 10, 15, or more, depending on the size of your deep fryer or pot.
  8. Don't make the puff-puff ball too big. The inside of an oversize puff-puff will be undercooked.
  9. When unsure, open up one puff-puff from the first batch to check that it is well done.
